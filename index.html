Hierarchy of Controls

Elimination

This means removing the hazard entirely. For example, if cluttered walkways are a problem, remove the clutter.

Substitution

Replace the hazardous item or process with a safer alternative. In a dental setting, this could mean using single-dose dental materials instead of bulk supplies that require manual dispensing.

Engineering Controls

These involve modifying the workspace or equipment to reduce or prevent exposure to hazards. Examples include ergonomic dental stools, preset chair controls, and proper ventilation systems.

Administrative Controls

These are changes to work practices that minimise exposure to hazards. They include training, job rotation, scheduled breaks, and limiting access to hazardous areas.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

This is the last line of defence, including gloves, masks, eye protection, and hearing protection. PPE should be used in conjunction with other controls, not as the sole solution.
